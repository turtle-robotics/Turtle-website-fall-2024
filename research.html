BLNC

This project is focused on developing robots capable of balancing like an inverted pendulum and BB-8. We will be exploring various control systems for a 2 wheel inverted pendulum and designing a ball-balancing robot. The goal of this project is to explore the capabilities of various architectures of balancing robots.

Looking For:

  • Controls or willingness to learn controls
  • Mechanical design and SolidWorks CAD
  • Arduino (C++ programming)

HATCHLING

The hatchling project is a semester long beginner friendly robotics project. At the end of the semester, students compete with their robots for a prize at the semi-annual TURTLE meetings!

Looking For:

  • Students with little/no experience in the field of robotics and engineering
  • Students looking to freshen up on things like CAD, embedded programming, and electronics

LARM

LARM, or Laboratory Assisting Robotic Manipulator, strives to create a 6 degree of freedom robotic manipulator from scratch that can assist other TURTLE members in tasks around the lab (being an extra hand in soldering, etc.). This is a challenging project, so don't feel afraid to ask help from senior project leads and TURTLE leadership. Try your best, break things, and make progress!

Looking For:

  • Electrical/Computer Engineering Majors looking to contribute to robot hardware
  • Computer Science Majors interested in contributing to robot software. Experience in computer vision, robot kinematics/dynamics preferred.
  • Mechanical Engineering Majors interested in designing the manipulator and authoring control methods.

MAZE

This project is focused on building and programming a small robot that will be capable of autonomously navigating an unknown maze and returning a 3D map of the correct path through the maze.

Looking For:

  • Electrical/Computer Engineering Majors: Preferably with experience in hardware.
  • Computer Science Majors: Some Python experience preferred, Linux experience preferred (but optional).
  • Mechanical Engineering Majors: Any CAD experience.

OLSN

QUAD

The Quadruped Project is focused on building a robot capable of walking on four legs, much like a dog. This type of robot must balance and adapt to its surroundings. The goal of this project is to explore the capabilities of walking robots.

Looking For:

  • SolidWorks CAD and analysis
  • Controls modelling
  • Python programming

VIRT

VIRT is a project focused on building a mobile turret that uses a color detection computer vision algorithm on an FPGA to track and follow a target.

Looking For:

  • Computer Engineers with experience in low level programming, FPGA's, or computer vision.
  • Electrical Engineers with experience in low level programming, FPGA's, or computer vision.
  • Mechanical Engineers with experience in CAD and software.
  • Any engineers with the passion to learn the above fields and work hard to contribute!

POBS

This project focuses on designing and developing a buoyant positive submersible capable of underwater navigation and depth control. The submersible will utilize motor-driven propulsion to maneuver underwater and maintain its position at various depths; returning to the surface if power to the vessel is cut to ensure data preservation and environmental protection.

Looking For:

  • CAD Experience. We will be utilizing SolidWorks 2024. (If you are unfamiliar with SolidWorks, that is okay. We will provide resources to get you up to speed.)
  • AERO, ELEN, EVEN, MEEN, MSEN, MXET, OCEN majors, or anyone else who enjoys learning and is looking to expand their horizons! Your major will not be a determining factor.
  • Understanding in - or willing to learn - controls and fluid dynamics
  • Experience in Fluid Simulations is welcome. (SolidWorks, DualSPHysics, Blender, etc...; Not required)
